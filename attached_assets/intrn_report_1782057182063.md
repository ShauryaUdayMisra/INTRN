### What Was Tested

Every major clickable element on the homepage was driven through a headless browser. Flows tested:

1. Homepage load + full scroll
2. "I'm a Highschooler" → signup form
3. "I'm a Company" → company landing
4. "Get Started" → auth register

6. Blog page
7. Help & Support page

9. Auth guard (/dashboard unauthenticated)
10. Direct URL navigation for 8 routes
11. Mobile viewport (375px)

### Issues

#### ⚠️ Issue 1 — Replit dev error message showing in production

**Routes affected:** `/internships`, `/profile`, `/company`, `/about`

All four return a 404 that includes:

> *"Did you forget to add the page to the router?"*

This is a Replit scaffold development message. Any user who lands on these URLs — via a typo, an old link, or just guessing `/internships` — sees what looks like a broken, unfinished product. Needs a proper branded 404 page.

#### ⚠️ Issue 2 — Desktop navigation is effectively hidden

The hamburger menu (Browse Internships / Blog / Help & Support) sits at `left: -304px` off-screen on desktop — it's only toggled by a very small icon in the top-left corner that's easy to miss.

On a 1280px desktop:
- **Browse Internships**, **Blog**, and **Help & Support** are not visible in the navbar
- The only way to reach Blog and Help from the homepage is to scroll to the bottom CTA section, or type the URL directly
- Most desktop visitors will never find the nav

**Suggestion:** Add Blog and Help as visible links in the header on desktop, alongside Sign In / Sign Up.

#### ⚠️ Issue 3 — Logo on the auth page doesn't navigate home

On `/auth`, the "intrn" logo in the top centre has no click target back to the homepage. Users who want to bail out of the auth flow have to use the `← Back` link or their browser back button. Every other page on the site has a working logo → homepage link.

**Fix:** Wrap the auth page logo in a `<Link to="/">` or `<a href="/">`.

#### ℹ️ Minor — Copyright year is stale

Footer reads `© 2025 INTRN`. It's 2026. Quick one-line fix.

### What's Working Well

- **Signup form** is clean: first name, last name, email, password, confirm password. Split-panel layout (form + marketing panel) looks polished.
- **Login error handling** works: bad credentials show a red toast "Login failed / Unauthorized" in the bottom-right corner.
- **Internship search** (/search) loads 9 live listings with cards, skill tags, location, hours, and Apply Now buttons. Filters are present.
- **Blog** has real content — multiple articles, category filters, a featured post, and a newsletter subscribe form.
- **Help page** is complete: support email, 24hr response time, FAQ sections for both students and companies, and a Report Issue button.
- **Company landing** (/company-info) is a polished pitch page with clear value props and a "start your journey" CTA.
- **Auth guard** is working — `/dashboard` redirects unauthenticated users to `/auth`.
- **Mobile** (375px) renders correctly — responsive layout, readable text, working CTAs.

## 2. UX & Design Review — 21 Jun 2026

### Overall: Genuinely impressive for a Grade 11/12 project

This isn't a school project that looks like one. It's a live, deployed product with real internship listings, real companies, a blog with actual content, and a working auth system. Most people never ship something functional at all — Shaurya has a multi-page SPA with search, filtering, dual user types (student + company), and a content strategy. That's legitimately impressive.

### Visual Design

**What's strong:**
- The purple gradient brand identity is clean and consistent. It runs through the logo, CTAs, the auth page marketing panel, and the blog newsletter section — feels intentional, not random.
- The auth page split-layout (form left, branded panel right) looks properly designed — like Notion or Linear's sign-in page. A lot of developers never get auth pages to look this clean.
- Typography hierarchy on the homepage is clear. "Internships for Highschoolers" lands well as a headline.
- The internship cards on /search are genuinely good — image, badge, title, location, hours, skill tags, Apply Now. Looks like a real product.

**What needs work:**
- The homepage's three-icon row (Gain Real Experience / Learn New Skills / Build Your Future) uses generic icons that feel like placeholders. They don't match the quality of the rest of the design.
- The internship preview cards on the *homepage* (not /search) are large and photo-heavy, showing only 2 cards before cutting off. The /search grid layout is much better — worth pulling that card style into the homepage preview too.
- The "Explore Real Internship Opportunities" and "Ready to Start Your Future?" sections feel flat — plain white backgrounds with no visual separation. A light background tint, a dividing band, or some texture would add depth and make the page feel less like a long scroll of same-colour sections.

### UX / User Flow

**What works well:**
- The dual-path homepage (student vs company) is smart and clear. Two big buttons, differentiated immediately — the right call for a two-sided marketplace.
- The company landing page (/company-info) is well-written with real copy. It reads like someone thought carefully about what a company needs to hear, not like a filler page.
- The blog is a serious SEO and trust-building asset. The volume of articles for a student project is remarkable — it signals credibility to companies considering listing.
- Login error toast in the bottom-right works and gives feedback without breaking the flow.

**What creates friction:**
- **Hamburger nav on desktop.** On a 1280px screen, Browse Internships, Blog, and Help are hidden off-screen. A significant share of desktop visitors will see only the logo, Sign In, and Sign Up — and nothing else to explore. Nav needs to be visible at desktop widths.
- **Apply Now flow unclear.** The button exists on every internship card — but where does it go for an unauthenticated user? Does it prompt signup? Redirect? Drop you somewhere broken? The most important conversion moment on the site should have the most polished flow.
- **Post-signup experience unknown.** After creating an account, where does a student land? What does the dashboard show? For a marketplace, the post-signup experience is where retention lives — if it's empty or underdeveloped, students won't come back.
- **Company "post an internship" flow missing.** The /company-info page has great copy but the only CTA routes to the same register form as students. Is there a way for a company to submit an internship listing? If not, the marketplace supply side doesn't scale beyond manually curated listings.

### As a Project, Contextually

For IB Grade 11 → 12, the standout things:

- **It's deployed and live** — not localhost, not a Figma mock
- **Real data** — 9 live internships from actual companies (Chandrani Pearls, Ripples of Hope, etc.)
- **Two-sided marketplace** — student and company flows, not just a landing page
- **Content strategy** — blog volume suggests he understands distribution, not just product
- **Reasonable code hygiene** — auth guard working, API separation, React SPA structure

The gaps are what you'd expect from a solo student builder: a mobile-first nav not adapted for desktop, some pages not wired up, and the conversion/post-login flows being the weakest part. Those are exactly what a second pair of eyes (or a second team member) would catch.

### Top 5 Things to Improve

| # | What | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Fix desktop nav — make Blog + Help visible links at ≥768px | Most impactful discoverability fix; one CSS breakpoint |
| 2 | Wire up or remove broken routes (/internships, /about, /profile) | Currently show a dev error message |
| 3 | Tighten homepage middle sections — replace placeholder icons, add visual separation between sections | Closes the gap between "student project" and "real product" visually |
| 4 | Add a company "post an internship" flow | If companies can't self-serve, the supply side doesn't scale |
| 5 | Show the post-signup student experience | Most important retention surface — if it's thin, build this next |

**Overall verdict: strong B+ moving toward A.** The bones are real, the brand is coherent, and the content is genuine. The delta to "this looks like a proper startup" is mostly nav polish and filling in the conversion funnel after signup.

### Web3 — handled separately, strong component

The most innovative hardening opportunity — connecting Shaurya's Bitcoin Club work to INTRN through on-chain credentials — is designed out in full in a separate document.

**[→ INTRN × Web3: SBT Credentials + Governance Token Design](https://gist.github.com/srimisra/c2eadde6553d88e846363c4079a48b61)**

Short version: two contracts deployed on Base (Coinbase's L2):
1. **INTRN Credential** — a Soulbound NFT (non-transferable) minted when a student completes an internship. Co-signed by the company's wallet. Stored on IPFS. Permanently verifiable by anyone without needing to trust INTRN as a platform.
2. **INTRN Token** — an ERC-20 earned through participation (completing internships, writing reviews, referring students), never bought or sold. Gives holders governance rights over platform decisions.

No speculation, no ICO, no financial value claim. The SBT specifically solves the credential fraud problem (anyone can claim an internship on LinkedIn — an on-chain co-signed token can't be faked). This is a genuinely novel deployment in the internship space and coherently combines Shaurya's two main interests into one story. The Web3 Gist has the full Solidity, tokenomics, learning path, and SOP paragraph.

**Sequence:** build the product loop first (see below), then layer Web3 on top. The first mainnet SBT minted — Taha's credential — closes the full story.

---

### What to build next — Product

The current gap: INTRN gets a student to "Apply Now" but what happens after is opaque. The full marketplace loop needs to close in the product, not just in Shaurya's DMs.

**Priority order:**

**1. Student application dashboard** *(1–2 weeks)*
- Student sees every internship they've applied to
- Status: Applied → Under Review → Accepted / Not Selected
- Single most important retention surface — if students don't know what happened to their application, they don't come back

**2. Company application inbox** *(1–2 weeks)*
- Company logs in, sees incoming applications for their listings
- Can mark: shortlisted / rejected / placed
- When marked as "placed" → triggers the SBT mint (once Web3 is live)
- This is also what unlocks real traction data: application counts per listing

**3. Check-in system** *(1 week)*
- Shaurya is running Taha's check-ins manually. Build it into the product.
- After placement: scheduled check-in prompts at week 1, week 2, end of internship
- Student and company both get a nudge → log how it's going
- Positions INTRN as the operating layer around the internship, not just discovery
- Differentiates from every listing site (LinkedIn, Internshala, Handshake) that disappears the moment a student applies

**4. Company self-serve listing form** *(1–2 weeks)*
- Right now companies are onboarded manually via seed files in the codebase
- A self-serve "Post an internship" form changes the supply economics entirely
- Also unlocks a real number: "X companies have listed organically"

**5. Live impact counter on homepage** *(half a day)*
- Pull from DB: students registered, applications submitted, interns placed
- Display on homepage. Even early numbers are more compelling than nothing.
- Updates automatically as the platform grows — a signal that the product is live and active
